MPages Help Pages : Selecting the Range on the Pulmonary Summary Graph
This page last changed on Jul 23, 2010 by JP3026.

You can change the graphed data-points using the Pulmonary Summary MPage Graph view. Through a combination of defining the Y axis values in the Bedrock wizard for categories like Respiratory Rate, Oral Temperature, Oxygen Flow Rate, Mean Arterial Pressure, etc., and through selecting a time period on the master graph for the X axis, you can affect how graphs are displayed on the Pulmonary Summary MPage. For example, you can affect the results for the respiratory monitoring graph, and you can affect the data trends graph simultaneously by selecting a range on the master graph. To select a time period on the master graph (or to zoom in), follow the instructions below.

  1. Once you have displayed a patient in PowerChart®, click the Pulmonary Summary MPage tab on the PowerChart menu. The Pulmonary Summary MPage is displayed to the right of the PowerChart menu.
  2. At the top of the Pulmonary Summary MPage, click the plus sign (+) on the Respiratory Graph component to fully expand it. The system displays the master graph which contains a range for a seven day time period, and it displays the two subordinate graphs in the component view.
  3. To narrow or zoom-in on the seven day range, position your mouse over the master graph at the top of the Respiratory Graph component. The system displays a cross-hair pointer.
    Note
    The system default is the last seven days for the master graph range.
  4. Drag the cross-hair pointer over the time period you want. Typically, you would zoom in for a more detailed view (a single day might be an example), where the results would be graphed in closer increments (two hour increments for a single day). When you drag the cross-hair pointer over a range within the seven day period, the system highlights and displays the selected period in dark gray.
  5. Click the selected dark gray range. The system redisplays the master graph and its two subordinate graphs below it.
  6. You could zoom in even further by repeating Steps 2 and 3 on the master graph.
    Note
    In this example for a single day, you are graphing the trend and the data for a two hour period.
  7. If you want to return to the composite view for the default seven day range, click the Reset Zoom button at the top of the Respiratory Graph component. The system re-displays the master graph and the two subordinate graphs to match the default range.
