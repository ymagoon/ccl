The Outpatient Summary MPage is focused on providing a patient summary view for Ambulatory clinicians. This page also provides additional functionality allowing clinicians to take action directly from the summary page. The actions provided in the first phase include the ability to add allergies, add documentation (PowerForms, PowerNotes), and add new orders using the modal order entry window. This phase also supports user-based customization, allowing the end user to rearrange components on the page and define the expand/collapse default for each component.

The Outpatient Summary Mpage contains the following components:
Patient Information, Diagnoses, Problems, Allergies, Medications, Patient Histories including Past Medical, Procedure, Family History, Social History, Pregnancy History for female patients, Vital Signs and Measurements, General Labs, Diagnostics, Health Maintenance, Immunizations, Visits, Microbiology, Pathology, Documents, New Order Entry, Outstanding Orders, and Notes and Reminders.

Workflow

Now let's take a look at how the ambulatory summary might fit into a typical workflow. I have my patient's chart open and the first place I visit is the Ambulatory Summary page. I'll start by reviewing the problems, allergies, medications, and vital signs. I'll take a look at a recent note... and now I'm ready to see the patient. (click Home icon) In this case the patient is here for a sore throat, and upon physical exam has a very red and swollen throat with positive lymph nodes. It looks like strep, so I'll order a Beta Strep and go ahead and add azithromycin. I click Submit for Signature and that takes me to the Orders tab. From here I can attach my diagnosis to the orders, fill out any required fields, and sign the orders. I'm automatically taken back to the ambulatory summary. At this point I'm finished if I want to complete my documentation later, or I can go ahead and add my note now. I just click Add in the Documents section, and from here I can open my note and complete it.
